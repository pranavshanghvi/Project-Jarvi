## What CCAR is

The Comprehensive Capital Analysis and Review (CCAR) is a Federal Reserve
supervisory program that evaluates whether large bank holding companies
have sufficient capital to continue operating through periods of severe
economic and financial stress, and whether their capital planning
processes are sound. Firms subject to CCAR submit capital plans and are
evaluated against supervisory stress scenarios (baseline, adverse, and
severely adverse).

## What DFAST is

The Dodd-Frank Act Stress Test (DFAST) is a related, statutorily-required
stress test that projects a firm's capital, losses, and post-stress
capital ratios under supervisory scenarios over a nine-quarter planning
horizon. Results are published by the Federal Reserve.

## Relationship to Basel capital requirements

Both CCAR and DFAST assess post-stress capital ratios against minimum
regulatory capital requirements derived from the Basel III/IV framework,
including the Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) ratio, Tier 1 capital ratio,
Total capital ratio, and the supplementary leverage ratio.
